How it works

Plain English version of what you're looking at. No math degree needed.

What is this?

I built a small predictor for the 2026 World Cup knockouts. After each round I grab results, score every team still in, then sim the rest of the bracket 10,000 times. The numbers you see are how often each team wins the whole thing, or wins a specific match, in those sims.

How I score a team

Each team gets one strength number from five things mixed together: Elo (long-term level), recent xG form (are they creating chances), squad value (rough talent/depth proxy), betting odds (what the market thinks), and injuries (key players out hurt).

Win % vs bracket %

The table is tournament win chance. The bracket shows advance % for each knockout game (blue = favorite in that matchup, green check = already won). A team can be likely to beat their R32 opponent but still have a lower trophy % if the path ahead is brutal.

The sim

I don't pick one fixed path. I play out the bracket 10,000 times with randomness baked in (including some draw/pen vibes). Stronger teams win more often, but upsets still happen. That's why Brazil can top the table without being a lock.

When it updates

I run it after knockout rounds when results are in. Out teams go gray and drop out of the sim. Elo and form move based on what actually happened on the pitch.

What it's not

Not a crystal ball. Seven games max per team, football is chaotic, and the model misses stuff like tactics and vibes. Treat it as an informed guess, not gospel.
