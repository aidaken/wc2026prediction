How it works

Plain English version of what you're looking at. No math degree needed.

What is this?

I built a small predictor for the 2026 World Cup knockouts. After each round I grab results, score every team still in, then sim the rest of the bracket 100,000 times. The numbers you see are how often each team wins the whole thing, or wins a specific match, in those sims.

How I score a team

Each team gets one strength number from five things mixed together: Elo (long-term level), recent xG form (are they creating chances), squad value (rough talent/depth proxy), betting odds (what the market thinks), and injuries (key players out hurt).

Who they played matters

xG form is adjusted for schedule strength. Putting up big numbers against Qatar counts for less than the same numbers against Brazil, so teams from soft groups don't get overrated. Each team's detail panel shows the raw xG, the adjusted xG, and how tough their run was.

Win % vs bracket %

The table is tournament win chance. The bracket shows advance % for each knockout game (blue = favorite in that matchup, green check = already won). A team can be likely to beat their R32 opponent but still have a lower trophy % if the path ahead is brutal.

The sim

I don't pick one fixed path. I play out the bracket 100,000 times with randomness baked in (including some draw/pen vibes). Stronger teams win more often, but upsets still happen. That's why Brazil can top the table without being a lock.

When it updates

I run it after knockout rounds when results are in. Out teams go gray and drop out of the sim. Elo and form move based on what actually happened on the pitch.

What it's not

Not a crystal ball. Seven games max per team, football is chaotic, and the model misses stuff like tactics and vibes. Treat it as an informed guess, not gospel.

The dictionary

Every term the model uses, in plain English. Tap one to open it.

Elo35% of strength

A long-term strength rating, same idea as chess. Beat a strong team, your number goes up; lose to a weak one, it drops. I replay every result from a fixed pre-tournament starting point each run, so ratings are reproducible and can't double-count.

xG (expected goals)chance quality

How good the chances were, not what the scoreboard says. A tap-in and a 35-yard screamer both count as one goal but very different xG. It cuts through lucky bounces and hot finishing.

xG form30% of strength

Recent chance creation vs chances allowed, as a ratio: xG for ÷ (xG for + xG against). Above 0.5 means you're out-chancing opponents, below means you're getting out-chanced.

Schedule strength (SoS)adjusts xG form

Who you actually played. xG gets rescaled by the opponents' own xG profiles, so numbers put up against weak teams count for less. A multiplier above 1 means a tough run, below 1 means a soft one. Clamped so a 3-game sample can't swing it wildly.

Squad value15% of strength

Total market value of the squad (Transfermarkt), a rough proxy for talent and depth. Pricey squad usually means more quality on the bench when it's needed.

Betting odds20% of strength

What the market thinks, converted from odds into an implied win probability. The market bakes in stuff a model misses, but it also over-loves big names, so it's only one slice.

Injuriesmultiplier

Key players out hurt. Instead of a weighted signal, this nudges the final strength up or down a bit. A full-strength squad sits at 1.0×.

Strength scorethe blended number

The five signals mixed into one number per team. If a signal is missing for someone (no odds, say), its weight gets shared across what's left instead of counting as zero.

Monte Carlo sim100,000 runs

I don't pick one bracket. I play the whole thing out 100,000 times with randomness baked in, then count how often each team wins. More runs means steadier numbers (about ±0.13 points of noise).

Win %table

How often a team wins the entire tournament across all the sims. This is the headline number in the standings table.

Advance %bracket

Chance to win one specific match and move on. Shown on each bracket tie. Winning your next game is not the same as winning the trophy, the path ahead still matters.

STRENGTH_SCALEtuning knob

Controls how much a strength gap turns into a win-probability gap. Higher means favorites win more, lower means more coin-flips. Set to 0.68, tuned by backtesting past results.

Draw / pensknockout chaos

A chunk of knockout games go to extra time or penalties, which is basically a coin flip. The sim pulls those matchups toward 50/50 so upsets happen at a realistic rate.
